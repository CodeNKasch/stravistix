Notice: improved fitness trend accuracy with a heart rate monitor.

From now, heart rate based activities have their stress scores processed with Heart Rate Stress Score (HRSS) method. They were previously processed using the Training Impulse (TRIMP) method only.

The HRSS method is recommended over TRIMP because:
  • HRSS scores are scaled to be use with Training Zones. TRIMP had a scale missmatch with Training Zones.
  • HRSS scores can be directly compared to Cycling Power Stress Scores (PSS). They use same referential or scale.
  • HRSS scores can be directly compared to Swimming Stress Scores (SSS). They also use same referential or scale.
With this change your HRSS scores will be ~40-30% lower than TRIMP scores. Fitness and fatigue values will also drop in the same time. It's an expected and normal behaviour. Basically just think that TRIMP scores are on "another scale" and HRSS scores are on the scale to be compared with training zones and others PSS and SSS scores. And like TRIMP, HRSS also depends on exercise duration and intensity and provides a good indicator to compare commitment between activities.
warning You should setup your own Lactate Threshold Heart Rate (LTHR) in athlete settings for best results. (LTHR is basically the latest best average heart rate you can maintain for up to an hour.)
However you can still enable back TRIMP as default heart rate stress score calculation method in the feature settings by clicking the settings icon.
And a new fitness trend guide is accessible from help_outline icon. Reading this guide is also strongly recommended.
